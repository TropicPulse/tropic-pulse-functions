1. WHAT PULSEBAND IS (THE REAL DEFINITION)
PulseBand is the base acceleration, stabilization, and self‑healing layer of PulseOS.

It is the subsystem that:

keeps the browser warm

keeps the GPU warm

keeps the network warm

keeps the renderer warm

keeps the OS responsive

detects drift

heals drift

predicts stalls

prevents stalls

merges state

smooths performance

maintains timing

maintains concurrency

maintains responsiveness

PulseBand is the heartbeat of PulseOS.

Everything else — GPU dashboard, OS dashboard, AI dashboard, PulseNet, PulseMesh — sits on top of PulseBand.

PulseBand is the base layer.

2. THE ORIGINAL PURPOSE OF PULSEBAND
PulseBand was created to solve one problem:

Browsers stall, freeze, and drift because they have no warm state.

PulseBand introduced:

warm GPU

warm network

warm timers

warm loops

warm state

warm renderer

This is why PulseOS feels “alive” instead of “web‑app‑ish.”

PulseBand is the warmth layer.

3. PULSEBAND’S CORE SUBSYSTEMS (v1 → v5)

✔ PulseBand Core (transport + warm state)
