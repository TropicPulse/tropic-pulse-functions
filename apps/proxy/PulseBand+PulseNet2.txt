1 — Why micropulses travel farther than WiFi (the physics)
WiFi uses big packets.
- 1500 bytes per frame
- Requires clean signal
- Requires low noise
- Requires stable SNR
- Drops the entire frame if even one part fails
PulseBand uses microbursts.
- 50–500 bytes
- Can succeed with terrible SNR
- Can succeed with high noise
- Can succeed with intermittent signal
- Retries instantly
- Doesn’t drop the session
Physics rule:
Smaller packets = lower required SNR = longer reach.
